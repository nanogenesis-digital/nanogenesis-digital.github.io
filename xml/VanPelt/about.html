About Ellen Van Pelt

The Flemish author Ellen Van Pelt (1980) has published short stories in several literary journals and debuted in 2015 with the novel Drift. For more than 10 years, she worked as a psychologist. Her most recent publication is Deze wereld is geen ergernis waard (2020), her biography about the Flemish author and journalist Roger Van de Velde.

"Dauphin"

In the story "Dauphin", the first-person narrator is on holiday with her son in Djerba, Tunisia. They go on a daytrip with a ‘pirate ship’ to Flamingo Island. It is the first time she must fulfil the role of both mother and father on holiday, as her partner has recently passed away. During the trip, she is unexpectedly helped by Mustafa, who takes over some ‘fatherly’ tasks, like carrying her son on his back after he refuses to go any further during their walk on the beach. She manages to get through the day and is rewarded by the sight of dolphins on the way back.

About the writing process

Van Pelt wrote “Dauphin” in 21 sessions over a period of 13 days between late November 2020 and mid-January 2021. The writing process resulted in 18 logged writing sessions sessions, with a total duration of 15 hours, 19 minutes and 26 seconds, as well as 18 Word documents.
