About Roos van Rijswijk

Roos van Rijswijk (1985) is a Dutch author who debuted in 2016 with the novel Onheilig. She has published several short stories in a wide range of literary journals, including De Revisor, De Gids, Tirade, De VPRO-Gids, De Correspondent, DW-B, De Groene Amsterdammer, De SLAA, Aicha Qandisha, Das Magazin, Slang, De Optimist and Hard//Hoofd. In 2021, she published a collection of short stories De dwaler, which includes the short story “Zorgvlied”.

"Zorgvlied"

In “Zorgvlied” the first-person narrator takes a walk with her mother through the Zorgvlied cemetery in Amsterdam during one of the COVID-19 lockdowns. During the walk, they are first accompanied by the ghost of her grandmother, after which her great-grandmother and great-great-grandmother also join them. The narrator can see and converse with them, but her mother cannot. This is, however, not the first time she has perceived ghosts; her grandmother has visited her before.

About the writing process

Van Rijswijk wrote “Zorgvlied” in eight sessions across five days in December 2020. The writing process had a total duration of 11 hours, 48 minutes and 1 second and resulted in seven Word documents accompanied by the keystroke data.
